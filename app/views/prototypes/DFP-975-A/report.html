Recommendations

Each document check is not suspicious alone and should be cross-referenced with other document checks and claimant information to identify any unusual patterns.

Recommended actions:

  1. If any of the details are not visible or clear, request another version that shows the full detail.
  2. Check for inconsistencies within headers, addresses, signatures and totals, paying particular attention to the first page. These are frequently altered areas.
  3. Compare names, dates and amounts for consistency across pages.
  4. Confirm incoming and outgoing totals add up. Sudden jumps may indicate something was removed.
  5. Select all text to find any empty selections that may show where content has been covered or replaced.
  6. Check if the created and modified fields in the document's properties make sense for that document type. For example, a bank statement is unlikely to be created in Word or by a specific individual.
  7. Compare the document to any verified template you may have available for the same sender or institution.

If you see a pattern within these checks that leads you to suspect the document has been tampered with, refer the documents to the relevant investigations team or decision maker.
